Investment Thesis

BlueOval Battery Park Michigan in Marshall is approximately 20% complete with the 1.8 million sq ft main structure taking shape. Ford invested $3B (reduced from $3.5B) and will create 1,700 jobs (reduced from 2,500). Production of LFP batteries begins 2026 with 20 GWh annual capacity (reduced from 35 GWh). In December 2025, Ford announced the facility will also produce residential energy storage batteries as part of a pivot from large EVs to hybrids and smaller, affordable EVs. State incentives were reduced to $384-409M from $1B. CATL technology licensing controversy continues. (Sources: Ford Motor Company Jul 2024; Bridge Michigan Dec 2025; Electrek Jul 2024)
