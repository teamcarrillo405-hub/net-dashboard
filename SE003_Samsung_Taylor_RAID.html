Key Risks

Investment Thesis

Samsung Taylor represents a cautionary tale of megaproject risk. The $44B initial facility is 92% complete (as of Dec 2025) structurally, but equipment installation has stalled as Samsung conducts a strategic review of all overseas investments amid corporate restructuring. The expanded $44B commitment appears increasingly uncertain. Samsung's foundry business has struggled with yields and customer acquisition, losing ground to TSMC. The Taylor facility risks becoming a stranded asset if Samsung decides to exit or scale back. The $6.4B CHIPS preliminary award provides some incentive to continue, but Samsung's commitment remains questionable. Until Samsung provides clarity on its strategic direction, the project faces significant uncertainty. (Sources: Samsung SEC filings; Commerce.gov CHIPS Award; TrendForce Oct 2025; Texas Governor Office)
